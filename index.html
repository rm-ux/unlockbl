Full Guide: Unlocking the Bootloader on Xiaomi Devices

Prerequisites

  • A Xiaomi account logged in on your device
  • A Windows PC
  • ADB and Fastboot installed
  • Mi Unlock Tool
  • USB cable
  • Backup your important data
  • Charge your phone to at least 60%
  • For HyperOS devices, install the Xiaomi Community App

1. Apply for Bootloader Unlock on HyperOS Devices (Xiaomi Community App)

  1. Install the Xiaomi Community App.
  2. Log in using your Xiaomi account.
  3. Navigate to the "Services" section in the app.
  4. Submit a request to unlock the bootloader in the "Unlock Bootloader Application" section.
  5. Wait for Xiaomi’s approval, which can take up to 7 days.

2. Enable Developer Options

  1. Go to Settings > About phone.
  2. Tap MIUI Version or HyperOS Version 7 times to enable developer options.

3. Enable OEM Unlocking and USB Debugging

  1. Go to Settings > Additional settings > Developer options.
  2. Enable OEM Unlocking and USB Debugging.

4. Link Xiaomi Account to Mi Unlock Status

  1. In Developer Options, tap Mi Unlock status.
  2. Tap Add account and device and ensure your Xiaomi account is logged in.

5. Install ADB, Fastboot, and Drivers

  1. Download and install Minimal ADB and Fastboot or the official Google Platform Tools.
  2. If needed, install the Xiaomi USB Drivers.

6. Download Mi Unlock Tool

  1. Download the official Mi Unlock Tool from Xiaomi’s website.
  2. Extract and install it on your PC.

7. Boot into Fastboot Mode

  1. Power off your Xiaomi device.
  2. Hold Volume Down + Power to enter Fastboot Mode.
  3. Connect your phone to the PC using a USB cable.

8. Unlock the Bootloader Using Mi Unlock Tool

  1. Open the Mi Unlock Tool and sign in with the same Xiaomi account used on your phone.
  2. The tool will detect your device.
  3. Click Unlock and follow the instructions. For MIUI devices, a 168-hour wait might be required before unlocking.
  4. Once the waiting period ends, reconnect the device and click Unlock again.

9. Complete the Unlock Process

  1. Once unlocked, the device will reboot and reset.
  2. Set up your phone as a new device (all data will be erased).

Post-Unlocking: What's Next?

  • Install custom ROMs, root your device, or install custom recoveries like TWRP.
  • Keep in mind unlocking may void your warranty and disable certain security features.
